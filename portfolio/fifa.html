The Divided 2026 World Cup

A geographic story map discussing potential controversies with the 2026 FIFA World Cup

This “scrolly-telling” piece uses maps to discuss the upcoming 2026 FIFA World Cup, hosted by Canada, Mexico, and the United States, and any particular issues that may arise from it. I was motivated to tell this story as although the World Cup is one of the most unifying events in the World, particularly the United States' border and immigration policy makes this a rather divided World Cup as well as a geographic issue. I used ArcGIS Pro to construct cartographically sound maps, used Adobe Illustrator to improve the symbology and legibility of the maps, and then used ArcGIS StoryMaps to construct an entertaining story using visual storytelling techniques.
