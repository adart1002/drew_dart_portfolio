The Changing Landscape of College Football

A geographic StoryMap exploring college football realignment.

This “scrolly-telling” piece uses maps to tell a story about how TV revenue from media rights deals has led to the controversial conference realignment in college football, starting in the 2024 season. I was motivated to tell this story as I am an avid college football fan, which allowed me to tell a geographically focused sports story. I referenced cartographic visual variables that helped make the bivariate data between college sports conference locations and TV revenue easily accessible through a value-by-alpha bivariate map, which displays the geographical extent of each conference across the country, and how much revenue they make from their respective media rights deals. Visual storytelling techniques were used to help familiarize readers with the conference realignment issue. I used ArcGIS Pro to analyze US counties and their distance to college football towns and then used ArcGIS StoryMaps to finalize the maps and to tell the story.
