National Parks Choropleth Map

A choropleth map that explores the increasing crowding issues at National Parks in the United States.

This choropleth map analyzes which states have the most crowded national parks. Even though parks like Yosemite, Zion, Yellowstone, and Grand Canyon are in relatively remote parts of the country, they still get millions of visitors each year, leading to high crowds. As many people visit national parks to get away from people, this map can help guide people to which national parks actually have the lowest crowds, even if they might appear to be closer to major hubs of civilization. This map was created by synthesizing the data into a choropleth map using ArcGIS Pro, and then improving symbology and other design aspects through Adobe Illustrator.
